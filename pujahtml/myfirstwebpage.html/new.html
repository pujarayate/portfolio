Tuesday, September 19: A barrage of torrential rains spelt disaster for many parts of Gujarat since the weekend, swelling its Narmada river and inducing widespread flooding in the state. This led to the Sardar Sarovar Dam filling to the brim for the first time this monsoon season, endangering riverbank residents downstream as authorities began releasing water from its sluice gates. As intense showers continued to feed Narmada for the third consecutive day, 6 lakh cusecs of water had to be released from the overflowing dam. This eventually necessitated the evacuation of over 12,000 residents from low-lying areas, including the Narmada, Bharuch, Vadodara, Dahod, Panchmahal and Anand districts.
